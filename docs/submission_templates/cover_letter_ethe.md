## 1. Significance and Fit with ETHE

This manuscript develops a multidisciplinary theoretical framework and operational assessment model for evaluating digital platforms used for high-stakes language test preparation (IELTS, TOEFL) in higher education institutions. The study integrates Information Systems theory (DeLone & McLean IS Success Model), Computer-Assisted Language Learning (CALL) evaluation frameworks, and learning analytics theory into a single, formally validated model.

We believe this work aligns closely with ETHE's focus on technology-enhanced learning at the tertiary level, particularly regarding:
- Framework and model development for educational technology adoption decision-making
- Institutional governance and quality assurance of EdTech platforms
- Bridging IS and pedagogical perspectives in educational technology research

---

## 2. Methodology and Validation

The study employs **Conceptual Framework Analysis** (CFA; Jabareen, 2009) integrated with **Design Science Research** (DSR; Hevner et al., 2004) for validation. Three layers of formal validation are provided:
1. **Theoretical**: Coverage matrix cross-validation across 12 reviewed frameworks
2. **Mathematical**: Linear programming proof of gap persistence (Theorem 1)
3. **Computational**: Monte Carlo simulation under Multi-Attribute Utility Theory (*N* = 100,000; Theorem 2)

No primary data were collected from human participants; all validation proceeds through formal mathematical and computational methods. **No IRB approval is required.**
