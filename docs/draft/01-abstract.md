# Abstract  -  IS EdTech Main Paper

**Word count target:** ≤ 250 words  
**Structure:** Background → Gap → Method → Key Findings → Contribution/Impact

---

The proliferation of digital technology platforms for high-stakes language test preparation (e.g., IELTS and TOEFL) in higher education has exposed a major conceptual gap. Existing evaluation frameworks are fragmented: computer-assisted language learning (CALL) studies emphasize pedagogical aspects while neglecting information systems (IS) architecture, whereas IS models prioritize technical adoption but overlook language testing specificity. To address this disciplinary divide, this study constructs an integrated theoretical framework and assessment model using Conceptual Framework Analysis (CFA) and Design Science Research (DSR) guidelines. The resulting multidimensional model integrates Technology Architecture, Pedagogy Effectiveness, and Institutional Governance dimensions, grounded in the DeLone and McLean IS Success Model as a meta-theory. The framework is operationalized into fifteen specific indicators with systematic operational definitions. Theoretical relationships are mapped in a structural equation model with eight propositions. The framework's utility and necessity are validated mathematically: a linear programming proof demonstrates the persistence of coverage gaps in existing frameworks, while a Monte Carlo simulation (*N* = 100,000) under Multi-Attribute Utility Theory (MAUT) complexity penalties demonstrates the Pareto dominance of the proposed framework (dominance probability: 85.80%). A proof-of-concept inter-rater reliability test indicates substantial agreement (Cohen's Kappa = 0.7917), validating the operational clarity of the assessment rubrics. This study provides educational technology researchers and university administrators with a rigorous, evidence-based instrument for evaluating and adopting high-stakes test preparation platforms.

**Keywords:** CALL evaluation, EdTech assessment framework, DeLone and McLean model, conceptual framework analysis, higher education
